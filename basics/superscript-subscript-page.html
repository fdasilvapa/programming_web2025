Formulas

Water is a chemical compound composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen
atom, represented by the molecular formula H2O. This notation indicates that
each molecule of water consists of two hydrogen (H) atoms bonded to a single
oxygen (O) atom. The structure of H2O allows it to form hydrogen bonds, giving
water its unique properties such as high surface tension, excellent solvent capabilities,
and the ability to exist in all three states of matter—solid, liquid, and gas—under
natural Earth conditions.
